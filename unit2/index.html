How do they exhibit effective use of space, size, or balance?

    ZERO

    This website uses a symmetrical system of balance. The main navigation is a circle that fills the entire screen, and it adjusts its size as the user changes the size of the browser window. An image is placed in the center of the circle and corresponds to a node on the circle when the user hovers over it.

    Planned Parenthood

    This website uses a strong grid structure to create an asymmetrical design. The logo is prominently placed in the upper left area of the screen. Below it is a rotating slideshow with important headlines. The focal point, however, is the dark blue vertical band on the right side of the page. It's contrast against the rest of the page's light-toned design draws the eye to the most important information on the page.

What makes them similar or different?

    These two websites couldn't be more different.

    ZERO uses a stark palette of black and white. The only color occurs in the images of artists' works. The navigation is unique because it is a giant circle with nodes along the perimeter. This design is intended to emphasize creativity and unique approaches to art-making. Users are meant to relax and explore.

    Planned Parenthood uses a more pragmatic grid-based design to organize a lot more information. Users of this website want information that is easy to find and clear. This site also uses complimentary colors (orange and blue) to draw the eye around the composition.
